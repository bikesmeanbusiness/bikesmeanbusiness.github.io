Bikes Mean Business – Charlottesville

Welcoming cyclists boosts your bottom line.

Did you know...

  • Business owners grossly overestimate driving customers by 200%+. Studies in Berlin found retailers thought 22% of customers drove, but only 7% actually did. In Toronto, retailers thought 25% drove when only 4% did!
  • Protected bike lanes boost business! NYC found a 49% increase in retail sales on 9th Avenue after installing a protected bike lane. Salt Lake City saw 8.8% growth vs 7% citywide.
  • Cyclists spend more per month than drivers. A Portland State study found cyclists spent 24% more monthly at bars and restaurants – they buy less per visit but come 2-3x more often.
  • Bike parking is efficient! One car space = 8-20 bike spaces, serving more customers and maximizing your storefront potential.

Choosing the Right Rack

Best choice: Inverted-U or post-and-ring racks – sturdy, secure, and easy to use.

Why other racks don't work:

  • "Wave" or "Ribbon" racks: Only secure the front wheel, bikes tip over easily, can damage wheels
  • "Comb" or "Grid" racks: Bikes fall over, only hold front wheel, can bend wheels
  • "Wheel bender" racks: Named for a reason – they damage wheels and don't secure the frame
  • Decorative/artistic racks: Often impossible to lock properly, confuse users, waste money

Good racks allow: Two points of contact, U-lock to secure frame and wheel, support for various bike sizes.
